Ana Aslan

Here is a time line of Ana Aslan's life:

  • 1 January 1987 - Born in Braila, Kingdom of Romania
  • 1915 - Graduates Central School of Bucharest
  • 1922 - Graduates Faculty of Medicine
  • 1924 - Obtains her M.D. degree in cardiovascular physiology
  • 1952 - Delevops H3, also known as Gerovital, in order to treat aging and diseases affecting older people
  • 1976 - Together with Elena Polovrageanu, they invent Aslavital, a drug that delays skin aging
  • 20 May 1988 - dies in Bucharest, Social Republic of Romania

International distinctions for her research:

  • Member of the International Academy of Science, Munich and Co-President
  • "Cross of Merit"-Germany, 1971
  • "Cavalier de la Nouvelle Europe"-Italy, 1973
  • "Les Palmes Academiques"-France, 1974
  • "Honorary Foreign Citizen and Honorary Professor of Sciences"-Philippines, 1978
  • "Member Honoris Causa" Diploma of the Bohemo-Slovakian Society of Gerontology-1981
  • "Leon Bernard" Prize-1982
